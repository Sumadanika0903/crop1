Crop Disease Prediction

Cloud-based Crop Disease Detection and Yield Prediction using Deep Learning

This platform utilizes advanced Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) hosted on cloud infrastructure to provide real-time agricultural analysis. By leveraging Deep Learning algorithms trained on thousands of plant images, we can identify early signs of crop stress and disease with high accuracy, enabling farmers to take proactive measures and secure their yields.

About the PlantVillage Dataset

The models used in this application are trained on the PlantVillage Dataset, an open-access repository containing over 54,306 images. It covers 38 class labels spanning 14 crop species, including healthy and diseased leaves. Below are details on common diseases found in the dataset and their prevention steps.

Tomato: Early Blight

Symptoms:

Dark, concentric rings ("bullseye" pattern) on lower leaves; yellowing foliage that drops off.

Prevention Steps:
  • Practice crop rotation (every 2-3 years).
  • Stake plants to improve air circulation.
  • Water at the base to keep leaves dry.
  • Mulch soil to prevent spore splash.

Potato: Late Blight

Symptoms:

Large, dark water-soaked spots on leaves; white fungal growth on undersides during humidity.

Prevention Steps:
  • Use certified disease-free seed potatoes.
  • Eliminate cull piles (discarded potatoes).
  • Apply fungicides preventatively if forecast predicts blight.
  • Destroy infected vines before harvest.

Apple: Scab

Symptoms:

Olive-green, velvety spots on leaves; deformed or cracked fruit with scabby lesions.

Prevention Steps:
  • Rake and destroy fallen leaves in autumn.
  • Prune trees to open the canopy for sunlight.
  • Plant resistant varieties (e.g., Honeycrisp).
  • Apply fungicides during the growing season.

Corn: Common Rust

Symptoms:

Oval to elongate cinnamon-brown pustules on both upper and lower leaf surfaces.

Prevention Steps:
  • Select resistant corn hybrids.
  • Plant early to avoid late-season infection.
  • Monitor fields regularly for early pustules.
  • Fungicide application in severe cases.
